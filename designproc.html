DESIGN PROCESS


User Comes First


The success of my design is directly proportional to user satisfaction. If my design helps the user successfully and easily accomplish said task then and only then I have accomplished what I set out to do. At every stage of the design process I try to build mental models of how the user would percieve and react at this point.




Define

First I articulate the problem in detail and understand various needs of key stakeholders.To the best of my ability I build mental models of users and proceed to research user goals and requirements

Research

I gather information by conduting interviews, observations, participatory research, industry research and peer reviwed publications analysis. I firmly beleive in the philosophy “Data is King” and effective data gathering is crucial in my process.

Ideate

I prefer to brainstorm as a team using different design methods. I like affinity maps and empahty maps the most but also depending the problem use concept maps as well.

Design

Move to the design phase by building an MVP of the intended product. I firmly beleive in an agile model where the first design only contains the core product functions which must be tested before building more detailed models

Prototype

I work best by sketching out the prototype on paper before moving to Balsamiq or Axure. I like Balsamiq due to its sketch skin but I prefer Axure for prototyping for the Web

Test

In my opinion all designs are subective and may not accurately reresent user needs unless tested periodcially and using a variety of core users. I prefer to use THINK ALOUD protocal for testing by I have also performed Wizard of Oz based on the product requirements

Analyse

Accurate visual representation of testing results provides me with a detailed view on which aspects of the design are failing to meet user needs. Depending on the product I build inforgraphics and visualizations to better understand problems with my design. I like using simple charts that helps all involved easily understand and interpret the results. To be critical and objective about the design i ask myself the following questions:

Ø What will the user be doing while using my design?
Ø How will the user take notice of it?
Ø What is their next course of action?
Ø How often will they use this design?
Ø What happens when the design fails?

Be humble and iterate

I try to best to let go of my personal opinion when working in teams. I beleive we must be humble about our ideas and strive to grow as much as possible.Experience has shown me that each new idea helps the design evolve and creates a helps bring in a new dimension of understanding.
